2. Database as Symbolic Form was written 19 years ago now. What has changed? What hasn't changed? Did Manovich's predictions come true?


Lev Manovich's exploration of the opposition between database and narrative in new media delineates the dichotomy between the unordered, non-linear structures of databases and the linear, cause-and-effect trajectories of narratives. Central to Manovich's perspective is the recognition of computer games as exemplars of interactive narratives, where users actively shape the unfolding story through algorithm-guided tasks. Expanding upon this concept, one can envision interactive film and literature allowing users to influence plot developments, immersive theater experiences where the audience becomes integral to the storyline, augmented reality narratives that dynamically respond to physical locations and interactive art installations where viewer interactions contribute to the evolution of the narrative. Embedded in these speculative forms is a common emphasis on user agency, transcending traditional creator-audience distinctions and fostering a dynamic, collaborative approach to narrative construction within the evolving landscape of new media, thereby reshaping cultural engagement and storytelling paradigms.
